Classic Beef Chili Recipe

Description

A big bowl of piping hot chili topped with shredded cheese, sour cream, and Fritos is one of my ultimate cold-weather comfort foods. It’s something that I’ve loved since I was a kid, and I still can’t get enough of it.

While I’ve tried quite a few classic chili recipes, I still hadn’t managed to find “the one” until I came across this one long ago. Once I made this recipe, the search was officially over; it is, hands-down, the best chili recipe I’ve ever made. It is worthy of lamination and a permanent home in any recipe collection.

Ingredients

  • 1lb Ground beef
  • 1 yellow onion
  • 1 clove of garlic
  • 1 green pepper
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp brown sugar
  • 2 cans kidney beans
  • 2 cans diced tomatoes
  • 1 can tomato puree

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a large Dutch oven over medium heat until it is shimmering.
  2. Cook vegetables and spices: Add the onions, bell pepper, chili powder, cumin, cayenne, salt, and brown sugar. Cook until the vegetables have softened, about 7 minutes.
  3. Mix in the garlic: Stir in the garlic and cook for another 30 seconds.
  4. Cook beef: Add the beef and increase the heat to medium-high. Cook, breaking up the beef with a wooden spoon, until no longer pink, about 10 minutes.
  5. Add remaining ingredients: Stir in beans, diced tomatoes (with their juice), tomato puree and the remaining ½ teaspoonsalt.
  6. Simmer: Bring to a simmer, then reduce the heat so it maintains a slow simmer, cover and cook for 45 minutes.
  7. Uncover and cook: Remove the lid and continue to simmer for an additional 45 minutes.
  8. Season to tase: Season with additional salt and pepper to taste before serving.
